Teaching Philosophy & Methodology

A structured, high-energy philosophy designed to maximize student output and engagement.

My instructional design is built on the reality of the classroom. While I naturally love chatting with students and maintaining a highly energetic atmosphere, the learning environment remains strictly managed. I combine established teaching methodologies with deep expertise in phonetics and pronunciation, linguistics, and real-world classroom management.

Maximizing Student Talking Time (STT)

A core competency of my practice is drastically reducing Teacher Talking Time (TTT). I deploy systematic Elicitation techniques and Concept Checking Questions (CCQs) rather than lecturing. This forces learners to actively retrieve and apply morphological and syntactic rules independently, keeping them engaged and at the center of the learning process.

Adaptive Instructional Scaffolding

I utilize various techniques tailored to the specific cognitive processing limits of the learner. For foundational students, I provide high-resolution grammatical breakdowns and strategic input paradigms to isolate target structures. For more advanced learners (B1/B2), I utilize Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT). These lessons are structured around concrete communicative tasks where language operates as a tool to negotiate meaning and complete objectives.

Strategic L1 Availability

My Master's research (Kane, 2026) specifically investigates the impact of L1 Availability on adult learners during complex dictogloss tasks. Rather than adhering to an outdated "English Only" ideology, I utilize L1 Availability as a deliberate cognitive tool. Permitting the native language provides essential scaffolding that offloads cognitive burden, allowing students to engage in higher-depth, form-focused metatalk without becoming overwhelmed.
