Teaching Philosophy & Methodology

A structured, high-energy, student-focused methodology designed to maximize student output and engagement.

My instructional design is built on the reality of the classroom. While I naturally love chatting with students and maintaining a highly energetic atmosphere, the learning environment is also effectively managed through time and classroom management. I combine established teaching methodologies with deep expertise in phonetics and pronunciation, linguistics, and real-world teaching experience.

Maximizing Student Talking Time (STT)

A core competency of my practice is drastically reducing Teacher Talking Time (TTT). I use Elicitation techniques and Concept Checking Questions (CCQs) rather than lecturing. This forces learners to actively retrieve lexicon and grammar and apply morphological and syntactic rules independently, keeping them engaged and at the center of the learning process.

Adaptive Learner Scaffolding

I utilize various techniques tailored to the specific cognitive processing limits of the learner. For foundational students, I provide detailed focus-on-form and explicit grammar breakdowns and authentic input to isolate target structures. For more advanced learners (B1/B2), I tend to utilize Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT). These lessons are structured around communicative tasks where language operates as a tool to negotiate meaning and complete objectives.

Strategic L1 Availability

My Master's research at Universitat Rovira i Virgili specifically investigates the impact of L1 Availability on adult learners during complex dictogloss tasks. Rather than adhering to an outdated "English Only" ideology, I utilize L1 Availability as a deliberate cognitive tool. Permitting the native language in certain specific situations provides essential scaffolding that offloads cognitive burden, allowing students to engage in higher-depth, form-focused metatalk without becoming overwhelmed.
